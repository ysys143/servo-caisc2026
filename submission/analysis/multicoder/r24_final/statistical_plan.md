# Frozen analysis plan

The three vendor outputs are automated annotations under fixed inputs, not independent human judgments. The generation stage contains 84 calls (14 records x 3 vendors x 2 conditions); they are not treated as independent scientific systems. The later 84 judge calls are a separate automated measurement stage and are not pooled with generation calls.

For single nominal fields, report raw unanimity, Fleiss' kappa, and pairwise Cohen's kappa. For set-valued fields, report MASI-distance Krippendorff alpha, exact-set agreement, pairwise Jaccard, and per-label prevalence and raw agreement. A constant category has undefined kappa and is reported as `NA`, never as perfect reliability. Do not average kappa across heterogeneous facets.

Baseline comparisons use four prespecified axes: (1) failure-mode detection, operationalized only as the unique-reference-evidence recall defined in `scoring_contract.json`, with out-of-key citations reported separately rather than labelled false; (2) within-condition agreement on evidence selections and diagnostics and, for SERVO, final facets; (3) analysis cost under `execution_metrics_contract.json`; and (4) design-recommendation quality under the frozen rubric in `recommendation_rubric.md`. Checklist probes and their reference answers are scoring assets only: they are never included in generation prompts or response schemas. `scoring.py` validates all 14 keys and binds every score to the SHA-256 hashes of its key and generated output. Recommendations are generated as free text, without a disclosed action-code vocabulary.

Recommendation quality is evaluated only after all 84 generation outputs are frozen and the generation run has a matching completed protocol manifest. For each record and originating vendor, the baseline and SERVO recommendation sets are relabelled A/B with a fixed-seed concealed permutation. Each pair is judged independently by the two vendors that did not generate it. Which judge receives each orientation is independently randomized, and the other judge receives the reverse order; this prevents a stable judge-vendor position from determining orientation. Judges receive the same anonymous source packet, the linked diagnostics and evidence, and the two recommendation sets, but not the condition names, coding manual, originating vendor, manuscript claims, probe answers, or other judges' outputs. They score evidence support, diagnosis--remedy fit, actionability, success-check verifiability, scope proportionality, and set-level non-redundancy on the frozen ordinal anchors, and select A, B, or tie. No judge may evaluate its own generation. The acceptance hook rejects unknown recommendation IDs, unknown evidence IDs, or mismatched pair/judge identifiers.

Report each rubric dimension separately, the prespecified equal-weight mean as a compact descriptive summary, pairwise preference/tie rates, inter-judge raw agreement and weighted kappa, and disagreements. Compute condition differences within originating vendor and record; do not treat judge calls as additional systems or independent scientific observations. A sensitivity analysis reports results by judge vendor and excludes pairs without two valid judge outputs. There is no post-hoc rubric revision, recommendation deletion, or author adjudication of scores.

Compute paired within-vendor, within-record differences. Aggregate with equal lineage weight and fixed-seed 10,000-draw lineage-cluster bootstrap intervals. Report leave-one-lineage-out results and a sensitivity analysis that removes AI Scientist v2 while retaining the 2026 record. Do not headline p-values or impose a success threshold.

The checklist is author-curated from the same corpus and its evidence-ID recall is an omission proxy, not semantic failure-mode accuracy or a gold standard. A cited item outside that checklist can be valid. The recommendation judges are LLMs, so their scores measure automated rubric judgments rather than human usefulness, correctness, or downstream design improvement. A/B labels conceal the named condition, but wording, structure, diagnostic granularity, and recommendation format can reveal condition membership; this is label blinding, not guaranteed condition blinding. Results cannot establish source entailment, construct validity, human reliability, general prevalence, gate-calibration effects, independent diagnostic superiority, or real-world recommendation utility.
