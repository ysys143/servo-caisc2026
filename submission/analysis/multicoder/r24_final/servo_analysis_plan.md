# Servo-only recoding analysis plan

The production study contains 42 fresh-session Servo codings: 14 frozen source packets by three automated vendors. Six records (`R01`--`R05` and `R14`) are the core cases analyzed in the manuscript; the other eight are supplementary applications used to inspect scope, boundary cases, and possible counterexamples. The source record, not the model call or coded facet, is the analytical unit. Baseline generation, probe-recall comparison, and automated recommendation-quality judging are retired exploratory designs and are not part of this protocol.

Each scheduled coder--record cell contributes one accepted coding. A cell may receive one bounded fresh-session retry under the identical frozen prompt after a transport or strict output-validation failure; all failed and accepted attempts remain in the execution record. No failed content is repaired or merged, and a cell that remains invalid after its second attempt blocks completion.

The primary outputs are evidence-grounded validator-channel case records for the six core systems, a supplementary eight-record scope audit, and a disagreement log. Each coded channel must identify its target property, evaluator and evidence source, operational decision role, feedback path, independence, reliability evidence, fidelity, and exact source support. The analysis asks whether the final Servo vocabulary exposes distinctions that matter in the cited cases; it is not a population reliability study.

Raw unanimity, pairwise agreement, exact-set agreement, and chance-adjusted coefficients may be reported as secondary descriptive summaries for the fixed 14-record convenience set. A coefficient is omitted when its unit alignment or category distribution makes it misleading, and an undefined coefficient is `NA`. No coefficient, confidence interval, or threshold determines protocol success or validates the taxonomy. Calls and facets are not treated as additional independent systems.

Disagreements are retained and classified as source absence, source ambiguity, system-boundary ambiguity, rubric ambiguity, or coder error. Any editorial adjudication is stored separately and never overwrites the three original outputs. Low agreement is a substantive audit result rather than a failed run.

The analysis can support claims about case-level applicability, evidence traceability, and distinctions exposed by the faceted validator-channel representation. It does not establish population reliability, human reliability, construct validity, failure prediction, downstream design improvement, representative prevalence, or superiority over another framework. Cross-framework comparison is qualitative and uses framework-native definitions, explicit scope differences, and representative case crosswalks outside this coding harness.
