CALIBRATION RUBRIC v2 -- validator DECISION ARCHITECTURE.

The original `Vcalibrated` field conflated two distinct questions, which is why
blind coders and the author split on AI Scientist (Nature 2026). We separate them
into mechanically-codeable sub-constructs.

A validation layer is "calibrated" if its outputs have evidence of agreement with
the property it judges: a deterministic check, a formal/numeric oracle, a physical
or wet-lab measurement, or a benchmarked empirical metric. An LLM judge / LLM
reviewer is NOT calibrated. Human social acceptance (e.g., peer review) is NOT
calibrated.

A layer is "gating" if its output DECIDES one of: accepting/rejecting a result,
writing it to memory as a success, promoting it to the next experiment, or
releasing/claiming it as a finding.

Fields:
- V_present (0/1): a CALIBRATED automated validation layer EXISTS anywhere in the
  system, even if it is not the layer that decides acceptance.
- V_gating (0/1): the layer that DECIDES acceptance is itself calibrated.
  0 if acceptance is decided by an LLM judge, peer review, or human social
  acceptance; 1 if by measurement, replication, a formal/numeric oracle, or a
  benchmark metric.
- novelty_gate (calibrated_auto | biased_auto | human | predefined | none):
  what decides whether an output is NOVEL or SIGNIFICANT.
  calibrated_auto = an automated calibrated novelty check;
  biased_auto = an LLM judge;
  human = a human decides;
  predefined = novelty is fixed by a constrained task / hypothesis space;
  none = novelty is not gated.

Thesis under test: trustworthy closed-loop discovery tracks V_gating (and an
adequate novelty_gate), NOT mere V_present. No surveyed system has a
`calibrated_auto` novelty_gate -- which empirically grounds the paper's
"novelty is non-automatable" open problem.
